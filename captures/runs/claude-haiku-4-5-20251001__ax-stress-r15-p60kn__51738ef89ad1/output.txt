**Formula:**
$$\sigma = \frac{P}{A} = \frac{P}{\pi r^2}$$

where σ is normal stress, P is applied load, and A is cross-sectional area.

**Calculation:**

- Area: A = π(15)² = 225π mm²
- Stress: σ = 60,000 N / (225π mm²) = 60,000/(225π) MPa

Simplifying:
- 60,000 ÷ 225 = 800/3 ≈ 266.67
- 266.67 ÷ π ≈ 266.67 ÷ 3.14159 ≈ 84.9

**Answer: σ ≈ 84.9 MPa** (or exactly 800/(3π) MPa ≈ 85 MPa)
