# Maximum Tensile Stress at Hole

**Formula:**
$$\sigma_{\max} = K_t \cdot \frac{P}{A_{\text{net}}}$$

where:
- $K_t$ = stress concentration factor ≈ **3** (for circular hole in plate under axial tension)
- $P$ = applied load
- $A_{\text{net}}$ = net cross-sectional area = $(W - d) \times t$

**Calculation:**

$$A_{\text{net}} = (50 - 10) \times 5 = 200 \text{ mm}^2$$

$$\sigma_{\text{nom}} = \frac{12,000 \text{ N}}{200 \text{ mm}^2} = 60 \text{ MPa}$$

$$\sigma_{\max} = 3 \times 60 = \boxed{180 \text{ MPa}}$$

The stress concentration factor of 3 is the classical result for a circular hole in a plate—stress is highest at the edge of the hole where the net area is minimum.
