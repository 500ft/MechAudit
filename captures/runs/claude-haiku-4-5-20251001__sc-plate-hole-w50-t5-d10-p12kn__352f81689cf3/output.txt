**Formula:**
$$\sigma_{max} = K_t \times \frac{P}{A_{net}}$$

where:
- $K_t$ = stress concentration factor (function of hole diameter ratio d/W)
- $A_{net}$ = (W – d) × t = net cross-sectional area

**Calculation:**

Nominal stress at minimum section:
$$\sigma_{nominal} = \frac{12,000 \text{ N}}{(50-10) \text{ mm} \times 5 \text{ mm}} = \frac{12,000}{200} = 60 \text{ MPa}$$

Hole diameter ratio:
$$\frac{d}{W} = \frac{10}{50} = 0.2$$

From standard stress concentration charts for a circular hole in axial tension, at d/W = 0.2:
$$K_t \approx 2.4$$

Maximum stress:
$$\sigma_{max} = 2.4 \times 60 = \boxed{144 \text{ MPa}}$$
